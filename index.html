Biography

I am an incoming master student at Peking University, where I am co-supervised by Wentao Zhang and Weinan E at AAIS. I also closely work with Renrui Zhang.

Since June 2025, I have been a research intern at Kuaishou Kling AI. Previously, my work mainly focused on text-to-image (T2I) generation. I am now most interested in World Action Models (WAM), with a technical goal of building scaling laws for embodied and visual intelligence together with the very best people.

AI Researcher

I care about models that do more than generate pixels: models that understand action, world dynamics, and visual reasoning well enough to scale into embodied systems.

Thinking Notes

I keep a running notebook on research, technology, and long-term personal growth. A few recurring principles shape how I choose problems and collaborate with people.

Global First

Avoid being locally excited by a narrow subproblem. Start from the global question, identify the highest signal direction, and spend effort on choices that compound.

AI As Leverage

Treat AI agents as a way to expand reading, prototyping, critique, and iteration. Define the goal and evaluation clearly before execution, then keep a harsh review loop.

Learn From Strong Environments

Growth is shaped by the people, projects, and information streams around us. I try to seek excellent mentors, collaborators, and projects instead of staying inside familiar routines.

From Connection To Creation

The internet era was largely about connection; the AI era is increasingly about creation. I am interested in how models, content, culture, and physical products may become one design space.
